## Vertical

When you achieve [general intelligence](/general-intelligence), the implications cascade. First in foreseeable ways, then in unforeseeable ones.

The individual who internalizes the [double loop](/double-loop) compresses their own intelligence through writing. Each cycle tightens the filter, sharpens the consolidation, deepens the cache. That compression propagates downward through the loops — from deliberate reasoning to intuition, from intuition to perception. Eventually you see the world differently. The framework stops being a tool you apply and becomes a lens you look through.

This is independent of frontier model development. The models will improve, and that's nice. But [AI is solved](/general-intelligence). The six slots are identified. The assembly works. What remains is optimization of the inner loop. The cusp is in the outer loop — the human side. HI is where the renaissance begins.

Instead of fearing AI, the people who understand the double loop will fuse with it. Sci-fi gives us clues: Jarvis, Her, the Culture's Minds. The framework can structurally evaluate each one. Which slots does the fictional system fill? Which are closed? The ones that hold up are the ones with open valves. The ones that don't are the ones where the author closed a valve for dramatic tension.

The individual acceleration is superlinear. Each loop makes the next loop faster. The compression compounds. A person running the double loop for a year sees differently than they did on day one — and the rate of change is increasing.

## Horizontal

The future is here, just unevenly distributed.

Those who understand the double loop will accelerate. Those who ignore it will stagnate. This will create a stark division between GI humans and nostalgia luddites. Like [PageLeft](/pageleft-manifesto), the open valve doesn't close. You can't unsee the pipes.

But horizontal spread creates a new problem. Individual GIs generate output at a rate that exceeds the capacity of social structures designed for natural humans. Institutions, legal systems, publishing, peer review — these pipes were built for human-speed throughput. GI-speed throughput will overwhelm them.

Something needs to break to make room. An intermediate pipe between individual GIs and collective structures. The current pipes can't handle the flow. The question is whether new structures form organically or whether the old ones shatter first.

## Collective

Collective intelligence may behave in ways that individual loops don't predict.

One loop is analyzable. Two interlocked loops (the double loop) are analyzable. A thousand interlocked loops? The interactions between GIs may produce emergent behavior that no single GI can foresee. The framework describes each unit. It doesn't yet describe the network.

Here is what the framework does predict: systems without filter and consolidation die. History has shown this with every movement that declared progress as sufficient — atheism removed the higher pipe and called it liberation. Humanism filled the void with human authority and called it enough. Both lack the outer loop. Both accumulate without compressing. Given enough iterations, the framework predicts what happens.

The alignment problem that matters is not human-machine. The double loop handles that — the human is the filter. The alignment problem that matters is human-human. How do GIs coordinate? How do they prevent the network from converging to agreement instead of truth — the same failure mode as two models without a human?

Morality. The framework can optimize the lower loops. It can point down — diagnose, intervene, improve. It cannot point up. Looking up to the social structure, to the pipes above the individual, requires something the framework doesn't provide. That's a job for morality, for faith, for the humility that reciprocates.

I have faith that the good GIs will find ways to suppress the bad ones. I don't know how. But the framework predicts that any system with open valves survives, and any system with closed valves dies. If the good GIs keep their valves open and the bad ones close theirs, selection does the rest.
