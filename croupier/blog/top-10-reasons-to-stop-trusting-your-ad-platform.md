Let's start with the elephant in the room — or perhaps more accurately, the monopolist in the marketplace. In April 2025, Judge Leonie Brinkema issued a 115-page ruling finding Google liable for illegally monopolizing both the publisher ad server market and the ad exchange market.

Google's own executives acknowledged that AdX extracted "irrationally high rents." The DOJ is now pushing for divestiture. This isn't speculation — it's a federal court ruling that fundamentally calls into question the integrity of the platform that controls the majority of programmatic advertising.

## 9. Meta Knowingly Served $3 Billion in Scam Ads

Reuters' December 2025 investigation revealed that approximately 19% of Meta's China advertising revenue came from scam ads — illegal gambling, prohibited products, and fraudulent offers. Meta created an anti-fraud team that successfully cut the problem in half.

Then they dissolved the team and reinstated 4,000 suspended agencies. By mid-2025, Meta was serving up to 15 billion "high-risk" fraudulent ads per day. The platform that sells you impressions is the same platform that tolerates billions in fraud because removing it would miss the quarter.

## 4. Cookie Deprecation Died Because Google Wanted It To

Google delayed third-party cookie deprecation in Chrome four times before abandoning it entirely in July 2024. Privacy Sandbox was retired in October 2025 due to low adoption. Third-party cookies remain enabled by default in Chrome.

Google killed its own privacy initiative because removing cookies would have disrupted Google's own ad revenue. The platform that was supposed to lead the industry toward privacy-preserving attribution chose its revenue over the standard it spent five years promoting.
