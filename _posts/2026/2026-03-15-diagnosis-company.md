*Part of the [cognition](/cognition) series. Applies the diagnostic from [Diagnosis LLM](/diagnosis-llm) to public companies.*

Every public company is a self-recursive loop. Revenue in, product out, revenue in. The loop either compounds or decays. [The Natural Framework](/the-natural-framework) diagnoses which cells are functional and which are broken. [The Handshake](/the-handshake) predicts the consequence: a broken contract compounds rather than self-corrects.

Warren Buffett calls the functional loop a "moat." The framework names the moat's six walls.

## The six cells of a company

| Step | Contract | Healthy | Dying |
|---|---|---|---|
| Perceive | Reads environment accurately | Customer research, market signals, competitive intelligence flow in | Ignores signals, falsifies internal data, executive bubble |
| Cache | Stores and retrieves institutional knowledge | Knowledge survives turnover, systems of record work | Knowledge walks out the door with employees |
| Filter | Rejects what doesn't matter | Says no to most projects, kills bad bets early | Tries everything, no focus, no kills |
| Attend | Ranks priorities with diversity guarantee | Multiple revenue streams that repel each other | One product, one market, one bet |
| Consolidate | Updates procedures from experience | Post-mortems change process, playbook evolves | Runs the playbook from ten years ago |
| Remember | Persists durable output for next cycle | Brand, patents, culture, ecosystem compound | Each quarter starts from zero |

The dim cell test: count the cells that exist but don't meet their contract. More dim cells, shorter time horizon before the loop degrades.

## Attend is the moat

A company with functional Attend has diverse revenue streams that suppress each other through lateral inhibition. iPhone doesn't compete with Mac. Insurance doesn't compete with railroads. Each winner owns a different region. When one region contracts, the others persist.

A company with broken Attend has one bet. Film. Phones. Search ads. The single axis inflates until it's the only thing the company knows how to do. When the axis shifts, the company has no diversity to fall back on. This is Kodak, Nokia, and every conglomerate that "focused on its core business" until the core rotted.

The diversity guarantee from [The Handshake](/the-handshake): survivors are dissimilar. A healthy company's business lines repel. An unhealthy company's business lines cluster.

## Consolidate predicts self-correction

The critical diagnostic question: can the company fix itself?

Consolidate is the cell that writes procedures. A company with functional Consolidate updates its playbook from failures. Intel under Grove: "only the paranoid survive" was a consolidation event. The memory of near-death changed how the company attended to threats. The playbook updated.

A company with stale Consolidate runs yesterday's playbook in tomorrow's market. The procedures that produced success become the procedures that prevent adaptation. This is the [innovator's dilemma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Innovator%27s_Dilemma) named as a cell: Christensen described the symptom, the framework names the step.

When Consolidate is the broken cell, the company cannot self-correct. The procedure that writes procedures is the one that's stuck. The fix must come from outside the loop: new management, a crisis, an activist investor who reads the diagnosis.

## SOAP note format

The diagnostic follows the same structure as [Diagnosis LLM](/diagnosis-llm). For each company:

**Subjective.** What management says. Earnings calls, shareholder letters, public statements. What do they claim about each cell?

**Objective.** What the filings show. Revenue concentration (Attend diversity), R&D allocation (Consolidate activity), employee turnover (Cache durability), market share trajectory (loop compounding or decaying).

**Assessment.** Which cells are functional, shallow, dim, stale, or broken. Whether Consolidate can self-correct. The three [death conditions](/the-handshake#three-death-conditions) applied: broken step, closed loop, or decaying input.

**Plan.** What would fix it. Which cell to strengthen. Whether the fix requires internal initiative or external intervention.

## Three examples

### Berkshire Hathaway: all six cells functional

The framework incarnate. Perceive: reads every annual report, talks to managers, visits factories. Cache: Buffett's memory is the institutional knowledge, but the decentralized structure means each subsidiary caches independently. Filter: says no to 99% of deals. A single criterion: "wonderful business at a fair price." Attend: insurance, railroads, energy, candy, furniture, jewelry. Diverse. Repelling. No business line competes with another. Consolidate: the playbook updated. Young Buffett bought cigar butts. Munger taught him to buy quality. The procedure changed. Remember: never sells. Holdings compound across decades. The output of each cycle feeds the next.

Assessment: all cells functional. The loop compounds. The moat is the pipeline itself.

Risk: Consolidate is personality-dependent. When Buffett and Munger are gone, does the procedure that writes procedures survive? Greg Abel inherits the loop. Whether he inherits the Consolidate is the open question.

### Kodak: broken Attend, stale Consolidate

Perceive: functional. Kodak invented the digital camera in 1975. They saw the future before anyone. Cache: functional. Deep institutional knowledge of imaging chemistry and optics. Filter: functional. They knew which research to fund. Attend: broken. One axis. Film. Every revenue stream clustered around the same chemistry. No diversity. No repulsion between business lines. Consolidate: stale. The film playbook from the 1960s. "Razor and blades: sell cameras cheap, make money on film." The procedure never updated despite the digital signal arriving through Perceive.

Assessment: Perceive worked. Attend and Consolidate didn't. The company saw the future and couldn't restructure to meet it. The broken cells compounded: stale Consolidate couldn't update broken Attend. The fix required external intervention (bankruptcy, 2012).

Prediction the framework would have made in 2000: Attend is single-axis (film revenue > 70%), Consolidate hasn't updated since the 1980s. When the axis shifts, the company has no diversity and no procedure to create diversity. Structural short.

### Apple: functional but fragile

Perceive: deep. Design research, supply chain intelligence, developer ecosystem signals. Cache: strong. Institutional design language survives individual departures. Filter: aggressive. Apple kills more products than it ships. "Focus is about saying no." Attend: diverse but weighted. iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables, Services. Five axes. But iPhone is > 50% of revenue. The diversity guarantee holds, but the weight distribution makes it fragile. Consolidate: functional under Jobs (changed the company's playbook repeatedly: computers → iPod → iPhone → Services). Under Cook, Consolidate is operational, not strategic. The procedures optimize. They don't reinvent. Remember: ecosystem. Switching costs. Brand. The output compounds and locks in the next cycle's Perceive.

Assessment: five of six cells functional. Consolidate is the watch cell. Under Jobs, it was the company's strongest cell. Under Cook, it's operational Consolidate (improve the process) rather than strategic Consolidate (change what we attend to). The company runs the playbook well. Whether it can write a new playbook is the open question.

## The investment rubric

1. Read the 10-K. Score each cell: functional, shallow, dim, stale, broken.
2. Check Attend diversity. Revenue concentration by segment. Do the segments repel or cluster?
3. Check Consolidate activity. Has the company's strategy changed in the last five years? Did it change in response to evidence, or in response to a crisis? Proactive Consolidate is functional. Reactive Consolidate is shallow.
4. Check for the death conditions. Broken step: which cell? Can it self-correct? Closed loop: is the company ignoring its market? Decaying input: is the market itself shrinking?
5. The dim cell count is the diagnosis. Zero dim cells: compounder. One or two: watch list. Three or more: the loop is decaying. If Consolidate is one of the dim cells: the company cannot self-correct. That's a structural position.
